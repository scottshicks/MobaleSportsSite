SAVE THE DATES! MOBALE is proud to announce that we will be partnering with the University of New Hampshire to present the 2026 UNH Wildcat Sports Education Camp for the Blind.

Dates: Sunday June 28 – Thursday July 2, 2026
Location: University of New Hampshire, Durham, N.H. campus — overnight lodging in safe & secure dormitories with adult supervision
Who can participate: Boys & Girls aged 10–18 (through Senior in high school) who are blind or visually impaired from Maine & other New England states
Cost per athlete: $100 — includes housing, athletic facilities, meals & camp T-shirt. NOTE: Sponsorships are available for athletes from Maine and former MOBALE athletes from other states who may need financial assistance.
Funding: Generously supported by The Gibney Family Foundation (TGFF), keeping program costs affordable for all athletes. Please reach out if the program fee creates a financial hardship.
Registration Deadline: May 1, 2026

MOBALE will take a major role in developing and directing the camp using the familiar vigorous, instructional, and FUN model of the Sports Education Camp (SEC) that we have presented for the past 10 years in Waterville, Maine.

Competitive Sports:

  • Goalball
  • Wrestling
  • Track and Field
  • Disc Golf

Additional Sports May Include:

  • Rock climbing
  • Tandem biking
  • Fitness and strength training
  • Beep kickball
  • Yoga
  • Swimming

New & Exciting!

University of New Hampshire graduate students from the Master’s Program of Kinesiology and Adapted Physical Education will join the MOBALE staff as trainers and counselors, offering expertise that will both challenge and support participant athletes.

Camp Benefits:

  • Confidence-building

  • Leadership development
  • Self-advocacy skills
  • Orientation & mobility skills

  • Personal health and fitness habits
  • Experience a college campus environment

And, of course… FUN, FUN, FUN!

About MOBALE

The Maine Organization for Blind Athletic and Leadership Education (MOBALE) is dedicated to promoting sports and leadership opportunities for visually impaired youth. Through our annual Summer Sports Education Camp and year-round programs, we empower young athletes to develop skills, confidence, and leadership abilities.

Our Sports Programs

Goalball

A Paralympic team sport designed for blind and visually impaired athletes, emphasizing teamwork and spatial awareness.

Wrestling

Build strength, technique, and confidence through adaptive wrestling programs tailored for visually impaired athletes.

Disc Golf

Experience outdoor recreation with adaptive disc golf, combining skill development with nature exploration.

Archery

Develop focus and precision through accessible archery programs with specialized equipment and guidance.

Rock Climbing

Challenge yourself with adaptive rock climbing, building problem-solving skills and physical strength.

Tandem Biking

Experience the freedom of cycling through our tandem biking program with trained sighted partners.

Summer Sports Education Camp

Our flagship annual program brings together visually impaired youth for a week of intensive sports training, leadership development, and community building. The camp features:

  • Professional coaching in multiple adaptive sports
  • Leadership and personal development workshops
  • Inspirational guest speakers and athlete mentors
  • Team-building activities and social events
  • Volunteer and peer support throughout the week
